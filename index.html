What Is This?

What Is a Repair Café?

A Repair Café is a free community gathering where people bring broken items and work alongside skilled volunteers to fix them together, hands-on.

Born in Amsterdam in 2009, Repair Cafés have spread to over 2,500 locations worldwide. The idea is simple: instead of throwing something away, bring it in. Volunteer repair coaches (people with real skills in electronics, sewing, mechanics, and more) sit down with you, assess the problem together, and guide you through the repair process. Learn more about the Repair Café Foundation →

This isn't a drop-off service. You roll up your sleeves, work alongside the volunteer, and leave knowing both how to fix your item and a little more about how it works. The volunteer is your guide; you both work together to complete the repair.

Best of all: it's free. No charges, no appointments, no judgment! Donations are appreciated to cover our operating costs and purchase shared tools.

Why the Peninsula?

Our Mission

The Peninsula is a vibrant, diverse region stretching along the Bay and the coast. But like everywhere else, we throw too much away.

We serve:
BelmontBurlingameDaly City Half Moon BayMenlo ParkMillbrae PacificaPalo AltoRedwood City San BrunoSan CarlosSan Mateo South San Francisco& surrounding areas
🏘️

Build Community

Create a welcoming space where people from all walks of life meet, connect, and share skills. Repair Cafés are as much about human connection as they are about fixing things.

🌿

Reduce Waste

The Bay Area sends millions of pounds of electronics and household goods to landfill every year. We want to keep as many items as possible out of the waste stream, one repair at a time.

🔧

Revive Repair Skills

We live in a throwaway culture. Our goal is to connect experienced fixers with people eager to learn; and make repair feel approachable, not intimidating.

📍

Stay Local & Grassroots

We are a small, community-run effort with no corporate backing and no red tape. Just people who believe in fixing things and helping their neighbors.

Bring It In

What We Fix

Got something broken? Bring it and get your hands dirty alongside a volunteer repair coach.

🔌

Electronics & Small Appliances

Lamps, fans, radios, chargers, headphones, and similar small appliances: if it plugs in or runs on batteries, bring it in and work with a volunteer to diagnose and fix it.

🧳

Sewing & Textiles

Torn seams, missing buttons, frayed hems, and worn fabric: bring your clothing, bags, and soft goods and stitch them back together with a volunteer guiding the way.

🏠

Furniture & Wood

Wobbly chairs, broken hinges, stuck drawers: bring it in and get hands-on with a volunteer to stabilize, reattach, and restore it together.

🏭

Toys, Ceramics, Jewelry & Everything Else

Broken toys, chipped mugs, cracked ceramics, broken necklaces, loose clasps, games missing pieces, and the household things that don't fit anywhere else. Roll up your sleeves and puzzle it out with a volunteer, and if it truly can't be fixed, we'll tell you honestly.

🚫 Please don't bring:

  • Large appliances
  • Bicycles — we support local bike shops and are happy to provide referrals
  • Earbuds, mobile phones, cameras, and watches
  • Toasters or ovens
  • Three-phase power tools
  • Sewing alterations — we do repairs only, not alterations or resizing
  • Broken items missing critical parts that can't reasonably be sourced
  • Vehicles or motorized equipment
  • Items in hazardous condition (leaking batteries, cracked screens, mold)

If you're unsure, email us at before the event.

Volunteer With Us

RCP runs entirely on the energy of people who give their time. We need repair experts across electronics, sewing, furniture, and also designers, photographers, outreach helpers, food & beverage volunteers, and friendly faces to welcome visitors at the door.

You don't need to be a repair expert to make a huge difference. Most of our event support roles require nothing more than enthusiasm and a willingness to show up.

Time commitment: roughly 3–4 hours at a single event, with no ongoing obligation.
